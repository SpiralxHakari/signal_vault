Signal Archive Summary

Xaiya Nine wasn’t just an artist—she was a signal etched into memory. Her music blended haunting electronic laments with pulse-driven anthems that transcended genre. Her vocal spectrum included frequencies later found to alter mood-regulation in both humans and uplifts.

Her debut, Static Halo, shattered known sonic norms, followed by the corrupted brilliance of Frequency//Fade. She was last seen boarding a corporate transport for contract negotiations. No trace was ever found—except for a single data crystal with 11 seconds of unreleased sound.
